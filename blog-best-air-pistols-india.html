₹6,900 — Camstar Star Leo: the right first pistol

The Star Leo is what we hand to first-timers. Break-barrel simplicity, honest 480 FPS, 970 g of steadying weight, and the only recurring cost is pellets. If the budget is tight or you're not sure airgunning will stick, start here — resale demand is constant.

₹7,999 — Camstar Mauser X Matte: the zero-drama bestseller

The Mauser X Matte is Camstar's best-selling springer for a reason: the Gen 2 internals tightened the trigger, the matte finish hides range wear, and the classic Mauser lines look far more expensive than ₹7,999. Functionally it's a Leo philosophy — cock, load, shoot — with nicer execution.

₹24,000 — Camstar Star RX Gen 3: the category-changer

The Star RX Gen 3 is India's first CO₂ semi-automatic. No cocking between shots — 8 rounds per rotary magazine, four magazines in the box, up to 400 FPS. It's the only pistol here that lets you train rhythm: double-taps, timed strings, follow-up shots. It ships as a full kit (hard case, holster, CO₂, pellets) in five colourways.

Budget for consumables: a 12 g CO₂ cylinder (~₹110 in our 5-pack) gives 50–80 shots. Shooting 200 pellets a weekend costs roughly ₹300–400/month in CO₂ — the price of one movie ticket for a genuinely superior training tool.

So which one?

  • First airgun, tight budget: Star Leo. No contest.
  • Want the nicest springer: Mauser X Matte — best feel per rupee on this page.
  • Serious about pistol shooting: RX Gen 3. The magazine-fed semi-auto training loop is something the springers physically cannot offer.
