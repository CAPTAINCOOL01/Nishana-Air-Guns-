If minimum running cost matters more than quick follow-up shots, this may not be the right ownership pattern for you. Who the RX Gen 3 suits

A strong fit when you want

  • Follow-up target shots without breaking a barrel.
  • Four magazines prepared before practice.
  • A kit instead of separately sourced basics.
  • A .177 CO₂ pistol for supervised target use.

Think twice when you want

  • The lowest cost per shot; CO₂ is another consumable.
  • Operation across temperature changes without managing gas behavior.
  • A deliberately slow, single-shot routine.
  • A purpose beyond the model's listed about-3 J specification.

The semi-automatic action is the key difference. One 8-shot magazine is used at a time; four magazines provide 32 prepared slots across the set, not 32 shots inside the pistol. Faster flow makes disciplined muzzle direction, trigger-finger placement and magazine handling essential.

.177 pellets: buy the right ammunition

Specifications call for .177-inch (4.5 mm) pellets. Start with a reputable lead diabolo type recommended by the manual. Flat-head wadcutters make clean paper holes; a quality dome is another useful comparison. Buy a modest quantity, confirm reliable magazine rotation and consistency, then standardise.

Seat pellets flush without crushing the skirt; never force a damaged or tight pellet. Use only ammunition approved for the exact model—not assumed-compatible BBs or darts. See current pellets and CO₂ on the accessories page.

What 12 g CO₂ changes

The RX Gen 3 uses a 12 g CO₂ cylinder. Temperature and rapid strings affect pressure, so velocity and shot count vary with conditions, pace, capsule, seals and pellets. The current product FAQ's 50–80 shots per cylinder is a planning range, not a guarantee.

Install capsules only as the manual shows; never force the fitting. For an abnormal leak, stop, avoid escaping gas and follow the service instructions. Do not store a pierced cylinder unless the manufacturer permits it.

Magazines: 8 shots × 4

Each magazine accepts eight pellets; four are currently listed. Number them to isolate a recurring feed issue, and protect them from grit or bent edges. Do not reshape or lubricate one without the manual's direction.

Before insertion, use the safety as instructed, keep your finger outside the trigger guard and check pellet orientation. After eight shots, remove and inspect the magazine rather than assuming it is empty.

Performance: set the right expectation

The published figure is up to 400 FPS and about 3 joules. “Up to” is not a promise for every pellet: weight, gas temperature and system condition matter. Stable stance, sight picture, trigger press and a proper target/backstop still determine useful practice.

A safe session, from case to storage

  1. Prepare the lane. Wear eye protection, use an adequate pellet trap/backstop, and keep people, animals and fragile surfaces beyond the target out of the area.
  2. Inspect. Read the manual, confirm pistol and magazines are clear, check for visible damage, and use the safety as instructed.
  3. Load deliberately. Keep a safe muzzle direction and finger outside the trigger guard; install the capsule and magazine according to the manual.
  4. Shoot only the target. Treat the pistol as loaded, know what lies behind the target, and stop for abnormal sound or operation.
  5. Clear and secure. Remove the magazine, verify unloaded status, handle CO₂ per the manual, then lock everything away from unauthorised users.

Maintenance without guesswork

After use, clear the pistol, wipe it dry and remove debris from magazines. Store it away from heat and moisture. Use only manufacturer-specified lubricant and quantity; general oils may harm CO₂ seals, while over-oiling creates problems.

Do not dismantle a valve, probe blindly at a lodged pellet or improvise an O-ring. Record the symptom and magazine, then use the dealer's service route. The RX Gen 3 review adds context; the supplied manual remains authoritative.

Running-cost maths

Using the current Nishana figures as a transparent example—₹550 for five CO₂ cylinders, ₹450 for 300 pellets, and the product-page estimate of 50–80 shots per cylinder—the gas component is about ₹1.38–₹2.20 per shot and the pellet about ₹1.50. Combined, that is roughly ₹2.88–₹3.70 per shot, before shipping, dry-fire loss, rejected pellets or price changes.

The RX should be bought for its magazine-fed CO₂ experience—not because it is the cheapest way to put holes in paper. If the gas budget feels disproportionate to how often you will use that experience, pause before buying.

Delivery and warranty questions to ask

  • Is my colour in stock, and what is the delivered price to my PIN code?
  • When will it dispatch, by which trackable courier, and how is package damage reported?
  • Which ID/address documents are required, why, and through which secure channel?
  • Will the invoice name the exact model and seller, with manufacturer warranty card included?
  • The current Nishana listing states 12 months' manufacturer warranty: when does it start, what is excluded, and who handles carriage and claims?
  • What covers transit damage, missing items, a seal leak or defective magazine?
  • Are magazines, seals and qualified service available later?

The final buying decision

Buy the RX Gen 3 for its .177 pellets, 12 g CO₂ system and four 8-shot magazines. Budget for gas, expect temperature and pace to affect performance, and confirm the box, warranty and service path in writing. If that ownership pattern fits, continue to the live product page.

Frequently asked questions

How many pellets can the RX Gen 3 hold?
Each rotary magazine has eight pellet slots. Four magazines are currently listed as included, so 32 pellets can be preloaded across the four magazines; the pistol uses one magazine at a time.
Which pellets does the Star RX Gen 3 use?
It is specified for .177-inch (4.5 mm) pellets. Start with a reputable lead diabolo pellet and follow the manual; do not substitute BBs or other ammunition unless the manufacturer explicitly approves it for the exact model.
What CO₂ cylinder does the RX Gen 3 need?
Current specifications call for a standard 12 g CO₂ cylinder. Fit and remove it only as described in the supplied manual, and never force a capsule or fitting.
What should I confirm before ordering an RX Gen 3?
Confirm live stock and colour, final delivered price, exact box contents, invoice, document process, dispatch and tracking, written manufacturer-warranty terms, transit-damage procedure, returns and availability of magazines, seals and service.
