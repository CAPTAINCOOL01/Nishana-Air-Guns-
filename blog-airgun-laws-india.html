Legal guide

Are airguns legal in India?

Short answer: yes — most airguns sold in India need no licence at all. Here's the full 2026 picture: what Arms Rules 2016 actually says, the 20-joule rule, who can buy, and how to stay comfortably on the right side of the law.

Law & licence15 July 20267 min readBy Nishana Airguns

The one-line rule: under India's Arms Rules 2016, air rifles and air pistols with muzzle energy of 20 joules or less in .177 (4.5 mm) calibre are treated as exempt from the licensing requirements that apply to firearms. Every airgun sold at Nishana Airguns fits inside this exemption — that's a deliberate stocking decision, not luck.

Where the rule comes from

Air weapons in India are governed by the Arms Act, 1959 and the Arms Rules, 2016 (which replaced the older 1962 rules). The 2016 rules drew a clean line: low-power air weapons — those that cannot exceed 20 joules of muzzle energy — were moved out of the licensing net. Anything above 20 joules is treated like a regular firearm: licence, police verification, the whole process.

That 20-joule figure is why product pages here quote muzzle energy so prominently. A Camstar Star Matrix Gold produces up to 20 J — the legal ceiling. A CO₂ pistol like the Star RX Gen 3 produces roughly 3 J. Both are comfortably inside the exemption.

Who can buy one?

Airguns may not be sold to minors. Our courier partners verify a government photo ID at delivery. Supplying any air weapon to a minor is an offence, and an adult must supervise young shooters at all times.

Is it legal to buy an airgun online in India?

Yes. Because licence-exempt airguns aren't "arms" for licensing purposes, they can be sold by retail — including online — to adult buyers. A legitimate seller will still do three things: issue a GST invoice (proof of lawful purchase — keep it), verify age at delivery, and ship manufacturer-genuine product with a warranty card. If a listing offers none of those, walk away.

What you still can't do (and shouldn't)

  • No public carry or display. An airgun in public causes panic and can attract charges unrelated to the Arms Act. Transport it boxed, in a case or bag, directly to where you shoot.
  • No hunting. Shooting any wild animal or bird — including pigeons and squirrels — is an offence under the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972. Airguns are for targets, full stop.
  • No firing beyond your boundary. You're responsible for every pellet. Shoot only with a safe backstop on private property (see our home range guide).
  • No modifications to raise power. Pushing a gun past 20 J moves it into licensed-firearm territory. Don't.

State-by-state caution

The Arms Rules are central law, but local police can impose temporary restrictions (for example around elections or public events), and a few jurisdictions have at times asked dealers for additional record-keeping. If you live in Jammu & Kashmir or a notified disturbed area, check with local police before ordering. When in doubt, message us on WhatsApp with your pincode — we ship pan-India daily and know where couriers face restrictions.

Quick FAQ

Do I need to register my airgun anywhere? No — licence-exempt airguns need no registration. Keep your GST invoice as proof of purchase.

Can I take it to a friend's farm to shoot? Yes, transported cased and unloaded, with the owner's permission and a safe backstop.

Can I keep it loaded at home? Store it unloaded, uncocked, and out of reach of children. CO₂ guns: remove the cartridge after sessions (it also protects the seals — see maintenance 101).

Is a licence-exempt airgun still dangerous? Yes — it will injure at close range. Handle it with the same discipline as any firearm: muzzle down-range, finger off the trigger, targets with backstops.

This guide is general information, not legal advice. Rules can change and local orders can apply — when unsure, ask your local police station or message us before buying.
