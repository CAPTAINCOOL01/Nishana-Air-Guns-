Buying basics

CO₂ vs spring vs PCP.

Every airgun on earth is one of three machines. Pick the machine first and the model chooses itself. Here's how the three power plants actually differ — with running costs in rupees, not theory.

Buying basics15 July 20268 min readBy Nishana Airguns

Shoppers agonise over models when they should be choosing a power plant. Spring, CO₂, PCP — each loads differently, costs differently, and shoots differently. Get this one decision right and every product page suddenly makes sense.

Spring piston — the honest workhorse

How it works: cocking the gun compresses a steel spring (or a nitro-gas strut); the trigger releases it to drive a piston of air behind the pellet.

Running cost: ₹0 beyond pellets (~₹1.50/shot with Star Match tins). Nothing to refill, nothing that expires. One shot per cock — which is exactly why coaches love springers: every shot is deliberate.

Watch for: spring recoil is a quick two-way jolt, so springers punish sloppy holds (good for learning, humbling for egos). The nitro-piston variant — like the Star Matrix Gold — smooths this out and can stay cocked without fatigue.

Buy a springer if: it's your first airgun, you shoot casually, or you never want a consumables bill. Start at ₹6,900 with the Star Leo pistol or ₹11,999 with the Star ZXi rifle.

CO₂ — speed and fun per rupee

How it works: a pre-filled 12 g CO₂ cylinder screws into the grip; each trigger pull meters a puff of gas. No cocking — which enables the party trick: semi-automatic fire.

Running cost: ~₹110 per cylinder (₹550 five-pack) for 50–80 shots ≈ ₹1.5–2 per shot on top of pellets. Cold weather drops pressure — winter shooters notice slower shots on a chilled cylinder.

Buy CO₂ if: you want rapid follow-up shots, action-style training, or simply the most fun per session. In India that category is effectively one gun: the Star RX Gen 3, the country's first CO₂ semi-auto.

PCP — the precision instrument

How it works: an onboard reservoir holds high-pressure air you fill with a hand pump or cylinder. Firing is a valve opening — no recoil at all, no cocking effort, ~90 shots per fill on the Star PX.

Running cost: ₹0 per shot (air is free) — but there's an entry toll: the gun costs more, and you need a fill device. That's why the PXi Complete Combo (rifle + pump + scope + bag, ₹31,000) is the standard first-PCP purchase — it deletes the "how do I fill it" problem on day one.

Buy PCP if: you care about group sizes, shoot 25 m+, or want magazine-fed shooting with match consistency. It's the endgame platform; most shooters arrive here on their second gun.
