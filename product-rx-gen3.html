What you're actually buying

The Star RX Gen 3 is the pistol that changed what an Indian airgun can be: the country's first CO₂ semi-automatic. Load a 12 g cartridge, seat an 8-round rotary magazine, and every trigger pull fires — no cocking, no breaking the barrel, no rhythm-killing pauses. With four magazines in the box you load 32 rounds at the start of a session and just shoot.

Why shooters pick it: the double-action trigger is crisp and consistent, the illuminated rear sight makes 10-metre work easy indoors, and the third-generation frame has the heft (≈900 g) that steadies your hold without tiring your wrist. It's also 100% engineered and made in India — Lucknow, to be exact — so spares and warranty service don't involve customs.

Best for: rapid-fire training, trigger discipline practice, casual competition and back-garden target shooting up to 15 metres. Not intended for pest control or hunting.

Frequently asked

Do I need an individual arms licence to buy this?
The Star RX Gen 3 configuration sold here is .177 calibre with muzzle energy of approximately 3 joules, so it is licence-exempt under India's Arms Rules 2016. Buyer identity and residence verification, an invoice and any applicable local restrictions still apply.
How many shots does one CO₂ cartridge give?
A single 12 g cartridge gives around 50–80 consistent shots depending on pace and temperature (CO₂ drops pressure in cold weather). We stock 5-packs of cylinders for ₹550.
Is it really 32 rounds?
The pistol uses 8-round rotary magazines and ships with four of them — 32 rounds loaded and ready per session. Spare magazines are available for ₹6,499.
Which pellets should I use?
Any quality .177 (4.5 mm) lead pellet. Our Star Match diabolo pellets (₹450 for 300) are matched to Camstar barrels — add a tin to your order.
What's the warranty and who handles it?
