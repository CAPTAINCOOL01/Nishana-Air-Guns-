How-to

Your home range, done right.

Ten metres is the international standard distance — and it fits in most Indian homes. Here's how to build a range that stops every pellet, keeps neighbours happy, and turns a corridor into a nightly training habit.

How-to15 July 20266 min readBy Nishana Airguns

The 10-metre air pistol and rifle events at the Olympics exist precisely because this sport was designed for small spaces. A corridor, a terrace wall, the side of a garden — any of them becomes a real range with one non-negotiable ingredient: a backstop that catches every pellet, including the flyers.

The backstop is the range

A .177 pellet from the RX Gen 3 can penetrate improvised materials and ricochet. Never treat cardboard, drywall or thin wood as a safe final barrier. Your backstop stack, back to front:

  1. A solid wall (brick/concrete) as the final guarantee — never a shared or thin partition wall.
  2. A pellet trap: a steel-backed commercial trap, or DIY: a sturdy wooden crate packed tight with old magazines/rubber mudflap layers, angled slightly downward so pellets deflect down, not back.
  3. The target mounted on the trap face — never on the bare wall.

Use a commercially rated pellet trap suitable for the RX Gen 3 and follow its instructions. Inspect it before every session, stop using it when damaged, and never fire a “test shot” toward a barrier whose rating you do not know.

Targets that keep you coming back

Paper 10 m targets are the standard scorecard. Add reactive targets — spinners, knock-downs, chalk squares — because instant feedback is what makes practice addictive. Tin cans work; glass never does (shrapnel + cleanup + angry parents).

Layout by home type

  • Corridor / hall (apartment): shoot along the longest interior line toward an external brick wall. Lock the door behind the firing point so nobody walks the line. Even 6–7 m is productive pistol distance.
  • Terrace: shoot toward a parapet wall with the trap against it, angled so nothing can skip over. Mind sightlines — shooting visible from the street invites panic calls (see the sourced legal guide and check local restrictions).
  • Garden / farm: the luxury option — full 10 m, natural earth bank behind the trap if available. Still verify the over-shoot line: you own every pellet's final address.

The four rules (airgun edition)

  1. Treat every airgun as loaded — always.
  2. Muzzle points down-range or down. Never at anything you're not willing to hit.
  3. Finger off the trigger until sights are on target.
  4. Know your target and what's behind it.

Add the house rules: eye protection for everyone at the line (ricochets are real), adult supervision for any young shooter, guns stored unloaded between sessions (storage guide).

The starter shopping list

  • The Star RX Gen 3, stored and handled according to its manual
  • Star Match pellets (₹450 / 300) — two tins minimum
  • Pellet trap + paper targets
  • Safety glasses, silica pouch for the case
  • CO₂ shooters: a 5-pack of cylinders (₹550)
