Owner setup · First session

Your first 100 shots should teach you something.

Build a clean baseline at 5 m and 10 m, learn the four magazines, protect the CO₂ system and separate harmless variation from a fault worth reporting.

OwnershipPublished 18 July 2026Updated 18 July 20269 min readBy Nishana Editorial Desk
The first-session plan

Use the first 100 shots as a controlled baseline, not a speed test. Inspect before loading, number all four magazines, begin with deliberate 5 m groups, move to 10 m only after repeatable operation, and log capsule temperature, magazine number and any symptom. New sealing surfaces may settle during the first capsule; a continuous leak, jam or sudden loss of pressure is not normal break-in.

Who · How · Why

Who: Nishana Editorial Desk. Nishana sells the RX Gen 3, so the product link is commercial.

How: This routine translates the listed .177, 12 g CO₂ and four-magazine setup into observable checks. It does not invent a chronograph result, group size, capsule yield or factory break-in requirement.

Why: A written baseline helps an owner describe a real fault accurately and avoids treating every early flyer—or every leak—as the same thing.

Before shot one: prepare the evidence

Read the supplied manual before fitting a capsule or pellet. Set up a proper pellet trap and backstop, wear eye protection, remove other people and animals from the lane, and keep the muzzle in a safe direction throughout. The India airgun safety guide covers the four rules, storage and transport in detail.

Keep the invoice, order message, warranty card and box contents together. Photograph the pistol and accessories as received, including any transit damage. Do not dismantle, lubricate or adjust anything before the baseline unless the manual explicitly requires it.

  • Confirm the pistol, four magazines and barrel path are clear and visibly undamaged.
  • Mark the magazines 1, 2, 3 and 4 with a removable label; do not scratch them.
  • Use one known, undamaged .177 pellet type for the full baseline.
  • Let the 12 g CO₂ capsule reach normal room temperature naturally—never heat it.
  • Prepare large paper targets labelled with date, distance, magazine and shot range.
  • Keep a second capsule ready; the current 50–80-shot estimate is not a 100-shot guarantee.

What “seal break-in” should mean

A new CO₂ system can show small changes as sealing surfaces seat and the owner learns the correct capsule-fitting pressure. That makes the first capsule a useful observation period. It does not create a duty to fire through a leak, overtighten a fitting, add unapproved oil or dry-fire until something improves.

A brief sound at piercing can be part of installing the capsule as the manual describes. A continuous hiss after installation, gas escaping near your hand, frost at an unexpected joint, or an abrupt pressure collapse is different: stop the session, keep the muzzle safe, clear the pistol using the manual and contact support. Do not probe the valve or improvise an O-ring.

Do not “fix” the baseline

Changing pellet type, sight setting, grip, pace, distance and magazine at once produces noise, not evidence. Hold the variables steady, record the symptom and make only the adjustment the manual authorises.

The 100-shot progression

Shots 1–8

One magazine at 5 m

Load magazine 1 carefully. Fire a slow group on a large target with the same sight picture and follow-through. The goal is safe operation and a visible group—not a score or sight correction.

Shots 9–32

All four magazines at 5 m

Fire one eight-shot group from each numbered magazine. Note any repeated flyer, stiff rotation, pellet seating difference or unusual report against that magazine number.

Shots 33–56

Repeatability at 5 m

Repeat three slow groups without changing the sights. If the centre shifts as the capsule cools, pause five minutes and record whether it returns rather than immediately adjusting.

Shots 57–80

Move to 10 m when ready

Only move back after every shot is accounted for and operation is consistent. Use the same pellet, stance and pace. A larger group is expected from the added distance; look for pattern, not a promised size.

Shots 81–100

Confirm, do not race

Use the second capsule if the first shows a sustained decline. Finish with deliberate 10 m groups, then repeat one 5 m control group if you need to separate distance from a mechanical symptom.

How to read the 5 m and 10 m groups

At 5 m, ask whether all pellets land safely on paper and whether one numbered magazine produces a repeatable outlier. Do not judge the pistol from one unsupported group. Repeat the same hold and pace; if possible, have a competent second adult shooter produce a separate target without changing the sights.

At 10 m, expect the group to expose more of the shooter's sight alignment and trigger movement. A consistent group away from the aim point is a sighting question; a random group that tracks rapid firing may be CO₂ cooling; a single repeating flyer tied to one magazine may be a magazine or pellet-seating clue. These are diagnostic leads, not remote diagnoses.

What normal CO₂ behaviour can look like

Usually explainable

  • A small point-of-impact change after a fast string as the capsule cools.
  • Shot count varying with temperature, pace, capsule and pellet fit.
  • A larger group at 10 m than at 5 m.
  • Gradual decline near the useful end of a capsule.

Stop and report

  • A continuous hiss or visible escaping gas.
  • A cracked, displaced or visibly damaged seal.
  • Repeated rotation failure or a pellet jam.
  • Sudden unexplained power loss, loose structure or unsafe control.

“Normal” does not mean “ignore it.” Write down the shot range, magazine, pace and capsule condition. If a symptom repeats, a short video captured from behind the safe firing line may help the seller understand it. Never place a phone, hand or face beside the muzzle or gas path to record a fault.

CO₂ care during and after the session

  • Control the pace. Deliberate groups reduce capsule cooling and make comparison easier.
  • Never heat a capsule. Do not use hot water, sunlight, a flame, heater or enclosed hot vehicle to raise pressure.
  • Do not overtighten. More force is not a cure for an installation error or damaged seal.
  • Use only specified lubricant. General oils and excess oil can harm sealing materials or contaminate the system.
  • Follow the exact storage instruction. Do not assume a pierced capsule belongs in the pistol long-term; use the supplied manual's removal and storage procedure.

Your one-page session log

For each target, write: date and time, 5 m or 10 m, pellet brand/type, capsule number, magazine number, shot range, approximate room conditions, pace, group observation and any sound or feed symptom. Photograph the target beside its label. That record is more useful to support than “accuracy is bad” or “CO₂ finished quickly.”

After shot 100

Remove the magazine, verify unloaded status yourself and handle the capsule exactly as the manual directs. Wipe external metal dry, inspect magazines for grit or damaged edges, lock the pistol away and keep pellets, capsules and access keys under adult control. Do not clean the barrel or dismantle the valve merely because the counter reached 100.

Compare the first and last targets. If the groups became more repeatable as your hold settled, that is useful owner learning. If a numbered magazine or mechanical symptom remained consistent, keep the evidence and use the written warranty/service channel. Frequently asked questions

Do the RX Gen 3 seals need a 100-shot break-in?
No fixed shot count can make a leaking or damaged seal normal. The first capsule is useful for letting new sealing surfaces settle and for establishing a baseline, but do not accelerate that process with dry firing, excess oil or overtightening. A continuous hiss, escaping gas or abrupt pressure loss means stop and use the written support route.
Should the first RX Gen 3 groups be shot at 5 metres or 10 metres?
Start at 5 metres on a large paper target so every safe shot is easy to account for and basic loading or sight issues are visible. After repeatable groups and normal operation across all four magazines, move the same target and hold to 10 metres. Change only one variable at a time, and record the distance on every target.
Can one 12 g CO₂ capsule complete all 100 shots?
Do not plan on exactly 100 useful shots from one capsule. The current product estimate is 50–80 shots, while temperature, pace, capsule, seals and pellet fit affect the result. Keep a second capsule available, change when the point of impact or report shows a sustained decline, and never chase extra shots by heating or overtightening the capsule.
When should a new RX Gen 3 owner stop and contact support?
Stop if there is a continuous gas leak, visible escaping gas near the hand, a cracked or displaced seal, a pellet that will not clear by the manual's procedure, repeated magazine rotation failure, a loose structural part or abrupt unexplained loss of power. Keep the muzzle safe, unload as instructed, photograph the symptom and contact the seller before dismantling anything.
